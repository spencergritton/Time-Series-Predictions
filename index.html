Project Proposal

Evaluating the Efficacy of Modern Recurrent Neural Net Architectures on Time Series Regressions
Spencer Gritton

Project Information

The idea for this project is to explore popular recurrent neural network (RNN) architectures and their efficacy in creating accurate regressions on time series financial data (Stocks, Cryptocurrencies, Bonds, etc). RNNs are used on a wide variety of problem sets including: autonomous driving, speech recognition/generation, and others. The use cases of RNNs, faced paced nature of the field, and a desire to continue learning about the field are the primary drivers for my interest in this topic.


Dataset Details

The dataset will include normalized price and volume data from a variety of sources to cover different financial markets. I plan to use open-source datasets such as those available on kaggle.com which include a variety of historical prices and other relevant data for a variety of financial instruments. These datasets are large enough to be sufficient in evaluating the performance of each architecture against its peers.


Software Used

I plan to use a variety of software to create and/or replicate popular RNN architectures. The most important technologies are listed below:

  • Python 3.x
  • TensorFlow 2.x
  • Keras 2.3.x
  • Numpy 1.x
  • OpenCV 4.2.x
  • Matplotlib 3.2.x

Relevant Papers

I plan to take a holistic approach to learning about RNNs. This will include reading both early and modern papers on the topic so that I am able to better understand: RNN use cases, early machine learning approaches to solving time series regressions, and the modern advances to RNN architectures applicable to my problem set. Initially I plan to read the following papers:

Finite-state automata and simple recurrent networks (1989)

Authors: Cleeremans, A., Servan-Schreiber, D., and McClelland, J. L.

A Critical Review of Recurrent Neural Networks for Sequence Learning (2015)

Authors: Lipton, Z., Berkowitz, J., and Elkan, C.

Empirical Evaluation of Gated Recurrent Neural Networks on Sequence Modeling (2014)

Authors: Chung, J., Gulcehre, C., Bengio, Y., and Cho, K.

Final Due Date
